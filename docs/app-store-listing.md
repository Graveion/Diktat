## Description (4000 char max)
You're on the train and you remember the bug you meant to fix before you left. Open Diktat, tell the coding agent on your Mac what's wrong, and it starts working — on your machine, in your repo, signed in with the tools you already pay for.

Diktat is a remote control for the AI coding CLIs you already run. Pair your Mac once, then start and steer sessions from anywhere: dictate a change, watch the diffs come in, get a notification when the run is done.

ONE APP FOR EVERY AGENT
Most of these tools only talk to their own. Diktat works with Claude Code, Cursor, Codex, GitHub Copilot and Kiro, so you can switch agents without switching apps — or juggle a couple at once across different projects.

IT RUNS ON YOUR MACHINE, NOT OURS
The agent runs locally on your Mac, using your own logins and subscriptions. Your code never lands on our servers. All we do is pass messages between your phone and your Mac, encrypted on the way.

TALK TO IT, OR TYPE
Dictation is built in. Say what you want changed, glance at the transcript, send. When you'd rather keep it quiet, type instead — same composer either way.

WATCH IT WORK
Output, file diffs and command results stream to your phone as the agent goes. A push lands the moment a run finishes, so you're not sitting there watching a spinner.

WHAT YOU NEED
- A Mac on Apple Silicon with at least one supported coding CLI installed and signed in
- The Diktat helper, installed on your Mac with one command (it's free and open source)
- Each agent's own subscription (Claude, Cursor and so on). Diktat doesn't resell anyone's tokens.

PRICING
Everything's free for the first 7 days. After that it's a small monthly or yearly fee, and you can cancel whenever you like. There's a yearly option that works out cheaper if you stick around.

Diktat is built by a developer who got tired of being chained to a desk to babysit a long-running agent. If something's broken or missing, the support link is right in the app.
